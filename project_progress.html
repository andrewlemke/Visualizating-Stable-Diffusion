- Andrew Lemke & Lakshyana KC

Introduction

The standard visualization for diffusion models is capturing the image at several points along the diffusion. The visualization appears as noise being removed from some image. This visualization is nice to look at, but doesn’t clue in to the “magic” happening. The viewers aren’t informed as to how or why the noise is reduced, or how a coherent image is formed from pure noise. One strategy our group tried aims to discover what is present in the noise and model by quickly denoising the image at those intermediate positions. This rapid de-noising attempts to show what information is held in the model and visible noisy image.
